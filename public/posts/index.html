You won’t believe me. By the end of this you might consider me bitter or self-indulgent. Perhaps you’ll pity me. But you won’t believe me. You will think I’ve embellished this story, or that it is an allegory for some trauma that I haven’t overcome. You might wonder if I’ve built this as a distraction to bury my true feelings. But that is not the case. The truth is far less subtle. Take my claim plainly: Every word I write is true. Every phrase is intended to be taken as unadulterated reality. At either edge of the field, beyond the painted hectares, stand neon-yellow metal tongs bifurcated to the sky, as the sky rests bluer than the day it was born.
