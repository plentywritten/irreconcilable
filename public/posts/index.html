by James Cody Phelan edited by: A clamor rang through the morning quiet. Gulls sprung from their roosts above drystone homes and squawked in protest, their days ruined. One squabble of birds drifted over to investigate the commotion and spied a human, only just passing through the dying Koumaria shrubs bundled at the northern edge of the village. The human labored, dragging anger behind him. Down below, the Goodwoman Sonja knelt before an apricot sapling inspecting the plant for blight. She wiped smeary sweat from her eyes to get a clear look. One of the roots dangled limply but bore no mark of the dreaded rot. As she prepared to set the sapling in the earth, her attention snagged on the noise which had so bothered the seagulls. She left the promising tree, dusted off her dress, and poked her head out around the inlet of her garden wall in curiosity. Other women, stooped women with sun-crinkled faces, quit their sweeping to investigate the noise as well, their husbands and fathers and sons were out at sea, having left well before dawn for the morning catch.
