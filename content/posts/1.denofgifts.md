You won't believe me. By the end of this you might consider me bitter or self-indulgent. Perhaps you’ll pity me. But you won't believe me. You will think I’ve embellished this story, or that it is an allegory for some trauma that I haven’t overcome. You might wonder if I’ve built this as a distraction to bury my true feelings. But that is not the case. The truth is far less subtle. Take my claim plainly: Every word I write is true. Every phrase is intended to be taken as unadulterated reality. I write without exaggeration or creative license. The truth I present is true in the cosmic sense. Reality as it is; not as I wish or construe it to be.  

I would describe the shape of pain: the material thingliness of pain as communicable between us. I need to describe it. This thing between us. I mean this wholeheartedly and literally. I speak of a very tangible thing that exists in our physical world. It is pain as animated golem. It is an entity that exists as clay if clay strung as wire between our bodies. And I am overwhelmed by it. Its presence overwhelms me; a fact made more concerning because I can barely perceive its physicality from my perspective. I cannot see its full shape. I witness a four-dimensional form through measly length, width, and height. It is beyond me. How, then, to describe such a thing? For this, I need help. Lend me your ear; lend me your hands. With them, we might staple together a crude signifier as we move closer to the form the words here are meant to convey. We would shape it between us.

Would you help me? Can you? Help me stack these words into existence, one by one, left to right, into a form. Triangulate from your vantage. Then mold its contours. Can you remember them before we whittle away the edges? Take a knife, peel away a layer if you would. Inch your blade closer. Slink it across the shape of the idea before us. Remember what we shave away and what we retain. That is your responsibility. See the whole picture, for I cannot. Make it whatever shape you like. Carve an elephant’s ear, or the crease of a hip. Make sense of the whittling strokes and appraise our creation. Judge the thing we make before you. Take it and hold it.
